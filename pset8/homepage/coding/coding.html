My journey to become a developer

After working in a tech company for so many years, the question has come up, "What else do you want from your work-life?" Management never appealed to me; the very notion was appauling. Somehow I chanced into a new-found friend who was a site-reliability engineer and through our friendship I slowly unravelled a possibility that was before me all along; I want to become a programmer as a trade.

Sadly, he wasn't supportive of my cause; seeing my other hobbies, he believes that I am artistic and should remain as such. Perhaps his gate-keeping comes from a good place; to save me from a mediocre life of failure and self-compromise. Yet I disagree; I love the puzzle and the challenge both. After years of taking calls from customers and feeling helpless in making a "real" difference, I found a tool to do just that. No longer will I cower in fear when I see a {} curly brace pair, and no longer will I fear Inspect Element on Chrome when I accidentally get into that mode.
